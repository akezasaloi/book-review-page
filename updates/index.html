About

Welcome to the review by The Page Turners, where we dive deep into the world of books and explore the stories that shape our perspectives. Here, we take a closer look at both Who Moved My Cheese? by Spencer Johnson and Born a Crime by Trevor Noah, two powerful reads that examine change, identity, and resilience.

Who Moved My Cheese? offers insights into how we respond to change, while Born a Crime blends humor with history to showcase the author’s experiences growing up in apartheid South Africa. Our goal is to reflect on the lessons, emotions, and truths these books share, and to encourage thoughtful discussions about their impact.

These reviews are brought to you by The Page Turners group, a passionate team of readers: Elizabeth Barongo, Hewan Mehari, Nebyat Haftay, and Akeza Saloi. Together, we share different viewpoints and experiences to bring richer, more meaningful reflections on every book we explore.

Born A Crime

Born a Crime by Trevor Noah is a memoir filled with humor and heart, detailing his experiences growing up in apartheid and post-apartheid South Africa. With vivid storytelling and a touch of comedy, Noah shares stories about his rebellious childhood, his unbreakable bond with his mother, and the challenges of living in a racially divided society.

“In my world, a woman was the most powerful thing that I knew. Still is.”

“People don’t want to be rich. They want to be able to choose.”

“Life is full of pain. Let the pain sharpen you, but don't hold on to it. Don't be bitter.”

Trevor Noah, Born A Crime

Who Moved My Cheese

Who Moved My Cheese by Spencer Johnson is a simple yet powerful story about adapting to change. Through four characters—Sniff, Scurry, Hem, and Haw. It explores how different people react to change, particularly in work and life situations. The book encourages readers to stay alert, embrace change, and move forward rather than resist it.

“What you are afraid of is never as bad as what you imagine!”

“Life moves on and so should we.”

“The quicker you let go of old cheese, the sooner you find new cheese.”

Spencer Johnson, Who Moved My Cheese
